Interactive MODIS NDVI & vegetation stress

NDVI (Normalized Difference Vegetation Index) measures how green and dense vegetation is in each map pixel: values run roughly from 0 (sparse or stressed vegetation / bare ground) toward 1 (thick, healthy canopy). Colors on the maps follow NASA’s GIBS MODIS NDVI palette; the bar under the maps approximates that scale (each pixel is matched to the nearest palette color to estimate NDVI for summaries). Data tables come from data/modis_ndvi_region_month.json. Use View for one region or compare two; use the month scrubber or play controls for the selected year. Maps: scroll or pinch to zoom, drag to pan when zoomed, then use Reset zoom to fit the tile again (zoom is kept when you change month or during Play / Resume; it resets when you change region, year, or View one/compare). Map tiles: GIBS WMS MODIS_Terra_L3_NDVI_Monthly; MODIS_Aqua_L3_NDVI_Monthly only for 2025-04-01 when Terra has no tile. Stress = share of pixels with decoded NDVI < 0.30; high health = NDVI ≥ 0.60.

Open this page via a local server (browsers block fetch from file://): python3 -m http.server 8080 then visit http://localhost:8080/interactive.html
